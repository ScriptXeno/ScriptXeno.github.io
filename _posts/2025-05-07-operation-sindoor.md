# Operation Sindoor: India’s Precision Strikes on Terror Camps After Pahalgam Attack

India's military has launched **Operation Sindoor** — a series of precision strikes on terrorist camps across Pakistan and Pakistan-administered Kashmir. The operation is a direct response to the **Pahalgam terror attack**, where 25 Indian civilians and one Nepali tourist lost their lives.

## 🎯 What is Operation Sindoor?

![Operation Sindoor](https://scriptxeno.github.io/2025-05-07-operation-sindoor-images/2.webp){: .shadow}

**Operation Sindoor** is a targeted military response by India to eliminate terrorist infrastructure located across the **Line of Control (LoC)** and in **mainland Pakistan**. The operation began days after the Pahalgam tragedy.

- **9 locations** were struck
- Targets were **non-civilian**, focusing on terror training camps
- The Indian Army confirmed **no Pakistani military facilities were hit**

## 🧨 Trigger: The Pahalgam Attack

![The Pahalgam Attack](https://scriptxeno.github.io/2025-05-07-operation-sindoor-images/3.webp){: .shadow}

The operation was launched in response to the **April 22, 2025** attack in **Pahalgam**, Jammu and Kashmir. A tourist bus was ambushed by militants.

- **26 dead**: 25 Indian citizens and 1 Nepali national
- Victims were mostly **Hindu pilgrims**
- Attackers fled towards Pakistan-occupied territory

This terror act shook the nation and was seen as a red line by the Indian administration.

> “We will hunt them till the ends of the Earth.” — *Narendra Modi, Indian Prime Minister*

## 🛰️ How the Strikes Were Conducted

![How the Strikes Were Conducted](https://scriptxeno.github.io/2025-05-07-operation-sindoor-images/4.webp){: .shadow}

The Indian Armed Forces planned the strikes with **surgical precision**:

- **Air and artillery units** used
- Focus on **quick execution** with minimal risk of escalation
- Avoidance of **civilian and military casualties**

Indian intelligence confirmed that the locations targeted were being used to **train and harbor terrorists**.

## 🧾 Pakistan's Reaction

![Pakistan's Reaction](https://scriptxeno.github.io/2025-05-07-operation-sindoor-images/5.webp){: .shadow}

**Pakistan has denied** any role in the Pahalgam attack and has **condemned India's response**:

- Accused India of targeting **civilian areas**
- Claimed it **shot down** an Indian drone and fighter jet
- Vowed to respond with “full force”

Pakistani media reported:

> - **26 civilians dead**  
> - **46 injured** in cross-border fire

## 🔥 India’s Stand: "Non-escalatory & Precise"

![India’s Stand](https://scriptxeno.github.io/2025-05-07-operation-sindoor-images/6.webp){: .shadow}

Indian defense officials reiterated:

> "Our targets were specific to terror infrastructure. We avoided military or civilian damage. This was a non-escalatory strike."

## 🗺️ Kashmir: The Perpetual Flashpoint

![How the Strikes Were Conducted](https://scriptxeno.github.io/2025-05-07-operation-sindoor-images/7.webp){: .shadow}

Kashmir continues to be a **hotbed of militancy** and tension:

- Root cause of **3 wars** between India and Pakistan
- Both nations claim it **in full**, control it **in part**
- Presence of **Pakistan-based militant groups** remains a major issue

Operation Sindoor is a bold yet **measured response** to cross-border terrorism. While the situation remains tense, India has made its intent clear: **terrorism will not be tolerated, and there will be consequences**.

As the global community watches, all eyes are on whether this act of retaliation leads to further confrontation — or forces both nations back to the negotiation table.
